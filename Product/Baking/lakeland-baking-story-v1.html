Top tips for better baking

1. Dont forget sifting

Flours are sold pre-sifted these days, so there's often no sifting required. The main exceptions are: flour for sponges needs to be sifted to aerate it for a lighter cake and flour mixes containing spices, cocoa powder & bicarb soda need sifting to ensure a good mix.

2. Perfect creaming

Creaming the butter and sugar (by beating them together until almost white in colour) before adding other ingredients incorporates more air and results in a lighter textured cake. Take care not to overbeat after you add the eggs & flour as this can make a heavy, coarse textured cake.

3. Correct cake pans

Recipes are designed for specific cake pans, so it's important to use the recommended size to avoid complicated recipe and baking alterations. Greasing and flouring or lining the pan with non-stick baking paper will prevent the cake from sticking.

4. Perfect temperature

An accurate oven temperature is vital, so if you do a lot of baking an oven thermometer is a good investment. For even cooking, centre the pan in the lower third of the oven so the top of the cake is in the middle of the oven.

5. Correct cooling

Cakes are fragile when they're removed from the oven, so leave them in the pan for five minutes before turning onto a wire rack to cool - don't leave it any longer or it will sweat and the crust will go soggy.

6. Icing made easy

Ice the cooled cake on a wire rack over a sheet of baking paper. Elevating the cake stops the icing pooling around its bottom, while the baking paper catches any drips. Work quickly or the icing will begin to set.
